Daxxify's Loyalty Play Is Coming for Allē's Throne

What's going on: Revance launched its Revvl Aesthetics rewards program this week, giving Daxxify its first real consumer loyalty infrastructure. Patients earn points per treatment that convert to dollar-off credits on future sessions — a direct shot at Allergan's Allē program, which has owned this space for years. Early pricing suggests the per-unit rebate could undercut Allē by 10–15% depending on your provider's markup.

What it means: Loyalty programs aren't just coupons — they shape which neurotoxin your injector stocks. If Revvl's economics favor the provider too, expect more offices to push Daxxify as a first-line option rather than a premium upsell. The longer duration (up to 6 months vs. Botox's 3–4) already made the math interesting; rebates make it compelling.

What's next: Ask your injector if they're enrolled in Revvl yet. If they're not, they may not have Daxxify pricing that reflects the new program. The enrollment window matters — early-adopter providers are getting better margins.

Ask your injector: 'Are you enrolled in Revvl, and does that change my per-unit cost for Daxxify?' The answer tells you whether you're getting the new economics or last quarter's pricing.

Collagen Peptides Passed a Real Trial and We're Cautiously Not Furious

What's going on: A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published this week in *Nutrients* found that 10g/day of specific collagen peptides (Verisol, if you want to check your label) improved skin elasticity by 15% and reduced wrinkle depth after 8 weeks. Sample size was 72 women aged 35–55 — small but methodologically clean, which is more than most supplement studies can say.

What that means: This doesn't mean every collagen powder at Whole Foods works. It means a specific peptide size at a specific dose showed a measurable effect under conditions where neither the subjects nor the researchers knew who got what. Check your brand's peptide source before you feel smug.

Lip Lab
That Viral Lip Oil Might Be Why Your Lips Are Peeling

What's going on: Dermatologists on social media flagged a spike in contact dermatitis cases this month linked to flavored lip oils — specifically products containing limonene, linalool, and cinnamal as fragrance components. Several TikTok-viral lip oils from mid-tier brands are in the crosshairs.

Why it matters: Flavored and scented lip products sit on mucous-membrane-adjacent skin for hours. Fragrance allergens that might be fine on your wrist can cause chronic peeling, burning, and micro-fissures on lips. The EU already restricts concentration limits for these allergens in lip products; the US doesn't.

The verdict: If your lips have been mysteriously dry despite constant product use, the product is the problem. Switch to a fragrance-free balm for two weeks and see what happens. Cerave Healing Ointment or Aquaphor — boring works.

Flip your lip oil over. If 'limonene,' 'linalool,' or 'cinnamal' appear in the last third of the ingredient list, that's your likely irritant. Swap it out for two weeks and watch what happens.
