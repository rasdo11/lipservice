The Injection Report
Allergan Just Blew Up Allē — And Your Points Strategy With It

What's going on: Allergan Aesthetics rolled out a restructured Allē loyalty program this week, shifting from flat-rate point earning to a tiered system that rewards higher spenders and penalizes one-treatment-a-year patients. Points per dollar now vary by annual spend threshold, and several promotional stacking options that power users loved are gone. The changes went live with minimal warning — most injectors found out the same day patients did.

What it means: If you were banking points across Botox, Juvéderm, and SkinMedica to fund a free syringe once a year, the math just changed. The new tiers essentially push you toward consolidating all your treatments with Allergan products to hit the spend thresholds where the earning rate actually beats the old system. It's a loyalty play disguised as a simplification.

What's next: Ask your injector where you fall in the new tier structure before your next appointment — and whether they're considering adding Revance or Galderma alternatives to their menu. Competition for your face just got more interesting.

Before your next appointment, log into Allē and check your new tier status. If you're below the second threshold, you may earn more value switching one of your treatments to a Galderma product with Aspire rewards instead.

Put It In Your Mouth
A Collagen Peptide Study That Doesn't Embarrass Itself

What's going on: A new randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published this week in *Nutrients* followed 120 women taking 2.5g of specific collagen peptides daily for 12 weeks and found statistically significant improvements in skin elasticity and hydration versus placebo — with dermatologist-graded and instrumental measurements, not just self-reporting. The peptides used were Verisol (types I and III), not the generic hydrolyzed collagen in most powders.

What that means: This isn't "collagen works" — it's "this specific peptide at this specific dose showed this specific result in a study that was actually designed correctly." Most collagen supplements on the market use different peptide profiles and doses that have zero clinical backing at their stated serving size.

This week's cheat meal: A proper lobster roll from wherever you are — Connecticut-style, warm butter, no mayo. It's the first real weekend of summer and you earned it.

What to cut: If your collagen powder doesn't specify peptide type and molecular weight on the label, it's fairy dust. Stop rebuying on subscription.

Lip Lab
Charlotte Tilbury Quietly Reformulated Lip Cheat — And Some Lips Are Mad

What's going on: Charlotte Tilbury's cult Lip Cheat liner appears to have been quietly reformulated in recent production runs, with users on Reddit and MakeupAlley reporting a waxier texture, reduced pigment payoff, and — in a handful of cases — contact irritation around the vermillion border. CT hasn't confirmed a formula change, but batch-comparison swatches tell the story.

Why it matters: Lip liners sit on some of the thinnest, most reactive skin on your body. Even minor changes to wax base, preservative system, or pigment dispersion can trigger irritant contact dermatitis — and "waxier" often means a shift from synthetic to natural waxes that carry more allergenic potential.

The verdict: If your current Lip Cheat pencil works, don't panic-buy backups of the same SKU — you'll get the new batch anyway. If you're noticing irritation, pause use and patch test. For the same overdraw-friendly precision, Make Beauty's gel liner is cleaner-formulated and nobody's complaining about it this week.

If you're experiencing redness or itching specifically at the lip line after using a liner, stop and patch test behind your ear for 48 hours before assuming it's just dryness.
