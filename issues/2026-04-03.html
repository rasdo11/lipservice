The Injection Report
Your Panoramic X-Ray Is Snitching on Your Filler

What's going on: A study published March 31 in *Dentomaxillofacial Radiology* analyzed 420 panoramic radiographs and found that hyaluronic acid filler in the nasolabial folds and lips showed up as distinct radiopaque masses in 23% of patients who'd had injections within the past 18 months. Radiologists flagged several as "pathology" before clinical history clarified.

What it means: HA fillers aren't supposed to be radiopaque, but cross-linked gels at higher concentrations scatter X-ray beams just enough to mimic soft-tissue lesions. If your dentist orders a CBCT scan, the detail gets even more obvious. This isn't dangerous, but it creates diagnostic confusion and unnecessary biopsies.

What's next: Tell your dentist you have filler. Seriously. One sentence saves everyone a referral.

Before your next dental imaging appointment, disclose every injectable you've had in the past two years. It belongs on your intake form now.

Put It In Your Mouth
Zinc-Carnosine Just Got Its Biggest Trial Yet

What's going on: A randomized, placebo-controlled trial out of Kyoto University, published online March 30 in *Gut*, gave 75 mg of zinc-carnosine twice daily to 196 adults with functional dyspepsia for 12 weeks. The treatment group saw a 41% reduction in symptom severity scores versus 18% for placebo. Mucosal integrity markers improved significantly by week 8.

What that means: Zinc-carnosine (sold as Pepzin GI in the U.S.) stabilizes the gastric mucosal lining without suppressing acid. This is the first large RCT to confirm what Japanese GI docs have prescribed for decades. If you're on a PPI you'd rather quit, this is the conversation to bring to your gastroenterologist.

This Week's Cheat Meal
A proper Japanese egg sandwich (tamago sando) from Konbi or your city's closest equivalent. Kewpie mayo, white milk bread, soft-scrambled eggs. The gut lining deserves softness this week.
Zinc-carnosine is not the same as zinc gluconate or zinc picolinate. The carnosine chelate is the whole point. Look for Pepzin GI specifically.

Lip Lab
Chanel Rouge Allure Velvet Got a Quiet Reformulation and the Internet Is Split

What's going on: Chanel updated the Rouge Allure Velvet formula as of its Spring 2026 batch (confirmed by component codes surfacing on Xiaohongshu and verified by Beauty Independent on April 1). The new version swaps synthetic beeswax for a plant-derived alternative and adds tocopheryl acetate.

Why it matters: The original's matte grip came partly from that beeswax-silicone matrix. Early testers report a slightly dewier settle and less feathering resistance at the lip line. Chanel hasn't acknowledged the change publicly.

The verdict: If you loved the old version's dry-down, buy your shade on markdown while batch codes still match. The new formula is fine, just different. It's a soft matte now, not a true one.

Check the batch code on the bottom of your tube at checkcosmetic.net. Codes starting with 6A are the new formula.
