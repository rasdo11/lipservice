The Injection Report
Allergan Pulls Juvéderm Vollure From the U.S. Market

What's going on: Allergan confirmed this week it's discontinuing Juvéderm Vollure XC in the United States, effective immediately. The Vycross-technology filler — a go-to for nasolabial folds and marionette lines — is being pulled for "business reasons," not safety concerns. Existing stock can still be injected, but no new orders are shipping.

What it means: Vollure sat in the sweet spot between Voluma (structural, longer-lasting) and Volbella (fine lines, lips). Injectors who used it as their workhorse mid-face filler are now pivoting to Restylane Contour or RHA 3, both of which have slightly different rheology — meaning they move and integrate differently under the skin. This isn't a like-for-like swap.

What's next: Ask your injector what they're switching to and why. If they say "it's all the same," find a new injector.

If you have a Vollure appointment on the books, call ahead. Your injector may still have stock — or may be switching you to a different product without a conversation. You deserve the conversation.

Put It In Your Mouth
High-Dose Fish Oil Is Giving You Bruises

What's going on: A study published this week in the *Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology* found that patients taking ≥2g of omega-3 fish oil daily had significantly increased bruising after injectable procedures — and slower resolution of ecchymosis by an average of 3.2 days. The effect was dose-dependent and persisted even when patients stopped supplementation 48 hours pre-procedure.

What that means: Your injector tells you to stop fish oil before filler. The new data says 48 hours isn't enough at high doses — you may need a full week off. This also matters for anyone bruising easily outside of procedures; your supplement stack might be the culprit, not your iron levels.

Lip Lab
The $9 Milani Lip Product That Just Outsold Charlotte Tilbury at Sephora

What's going on: Milani's new Color Fetish Hydrating Lip Stain hit Sephora two weeks ago and quietly climbed to the #3 bestselling lip product this week, beating out Charlotte Tilbury's Pillow Talk and Rare Beauty's Soft Pinch Tinted Lip Oil in the same period.

Why it matters: The formula uses a polyglutamic acid base instead of the standard hyaluronic acid found in most hydrating tints. PGA holds four times more moisture than HA and creates a film that actually improves wear time — so you're getting hydration that doesn't sacrifice longevity. At $9, the cost-per-wear math is obscene.

The verdict: Buildable, non-sticky, wears about 4 hours before fading evenly. The shade range is small (8 shades) but well-edited. Best for anyone who wants stain payoff without the dryness.

Polyglutamic acid holds 4x the moisture of hyaluronic acid and creates a longer-wearing film. Look for it on ingredient lists — it's starting to show up everywhere in lip formulas.
