The Injection Report
The 24-Hour Dissolve

What's going on: A study published this week in *Aesthetic Surgery Journal* tested a weight-based hyaluronidase dosing protocol that achieved near-complete HA filler dissolution within 24 hours across 87% of subjects — up from the inconsistent multi-session approach most injectors currently use. The protocol uses higher initial units with ultrasound-guided placement directly into the filler bolus.

What it means: Filler reversal has always been the messy part of the equation — unpredictable timing, uneven dissolution, sometimes eating into your own native hyaluronic acid. This standardized dosing model gives injectors a reproducible framework, which means fewer "let's wait and see" follow-ups and more predictable outcomes when something goes wrong or you just want it out.

What's next: Ask your injector whether they're using ultrasound guidance for dissolutions. If the answer is no, ask why. The study showed blind placement had a 40% lower efficacy rate at the 24-hour mark.

The key number: 150 units per mL of estimated filler, placed under ultrasound. That's the protocol. If your injector is eyeballing it, this study is the conversation starter.

Put It In Your Mouth
Cocoa Flavanols Finally Have a Real Trial

What's going on: The COSMOS trial follow-up data dropped this week in *The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition*, and it's the one the supplement girlies have been waiting for. 500 mg/day of cocoa flavanols over two years showed statistically significant improvements in skin elasticity and hydration in the 45–65 cohort — not self-reported, measured by cutometer.

What that means: This isn't a 12-person pilot with a chocolate brand's logo on the funding page. It's a large, randomized, placebo-controlled trial with objective skin measurements. Cocoa flavanols improve endothelial function, which means better blood flow to the dermis, which means your skin actually gets the nutrients you keep paying for.

This week's cheat meal: A proper Mexican hot chocolate from your nearest panadería — Ibarra tablets, whole milk, cinnamon, no apology. You're funding your endothelium.

What to cut: Drop the collagen gummies if you're adding a flavanol supplement. The absorption math doesn't work for both and your liver has opinions.

Lip Lab
Glossier Quietly Changed the Ultralip Formula

What's going on: Glossier updated the Ultralip formula sometime in the last two weeks — new batch codes show reformulated ingredient lists with castor oil moved up and the addition of squalane. They haven't announced it. Reddit caught it.

Why it matters: The original Ultralip was a balm-lipstick hybrid that dried down slightly tacky. Swapping in more castor oil and adding squalane signals a shift toward slip and moisture retention — basically, they're chasing the Rhode Peptide Lip Treatment customer who wants color but won't sacrifice feel.

The verdict: If you bounced on Ultralip because of the texture, revisit. If you loved the semi-matte finish, you might hate this. The shade Trench is still perfect. The formula is now a different product wearing the same name.

Check your batch code (bottom of tube, starts with a letter). If it's an 'M' batch, you have the new formula. Anything before that is old stock.
