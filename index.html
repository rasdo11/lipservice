The Injection Report
An Injectable That Restores Fat Instead of Replacing It

What's going on: Acumen Pharmaceuticals announced this week that the FDA granted fast-track designation to ACP-801, an injectable scaffold that stimulates the body's own adipocytes to regenerate facial fat pads lost to rapid weight loss — specifically the buccal and malar fat GLP-1 patients keep mourning. Unlike HA fillers, it's not adding volume; it's recruiting your own fat cells back to the site. Phase II enrollment opens in August.

What it means: Current solutions for Ozempic face are either filler (temporary, migrates) or fat grafting (surgical, unpredictable take rate). A regenerative scaffold is a genuinely different mechanism — closer to how Sculptra stimulates collagen, but targeting fat. If the Phase II data holds, this could change the post-GLP-1 treatment algorithm entirely.

What's next: Phase II results aren't expected until late 2026, so nobody's getting this tomorrow. But if your injector is already talking about fat grafting to address volume loss, ask whether waiting for non-surgical regenerative options is reasonable for your timeline.

Ask your injector: 'Am I a candidate for Sculptra as a bridge while regenerative fat injectables are in trials, or is my volume loss severe enough to consider grafting now?'

Put It In Your Mouth
High-Dose Fish Oil Just Got a Warning Label It Deserves

What's going on: A meta-analysis published this week in *The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition* across 14 RCTs (n = 71,000+) found that omega-3 supplementation above 2g/day was associated with a statistically significant increase in LDL cholesterol — roughly 4–7% in people already on statins. The cardiology internet is having a normal one.

What that means: If you're taking a high-dose fish oil stack for skin hydration or inflammation and you're also managing cholesterol, you may be working against yourself. Below 2g/day, the LDL effect was negligible. Dose matters more than you were told.

This week's cheat meal: The lobster roll at Luke's just dropped their summer limited — brown butter and chive on a brioche split top. Omega-3s delivered the way god intended.

What to cut: Any fish oil supplement protocol above 2g/day that your functional medicine practitioner prescribed without checking your lipid panel first.

Lip Lab
Dior's New Lip Oil Reformulated Away Its Best Ingredient

What's going on: Dior Addict Lip Glow Oil relaunched this week with updated packaging and six new shades. What the press release didn't mention: they quietly swapped cherry oil — the original's marquee emollient — for a cheaper blend of hydrogenated polyisobutene and synthetic esters.

Why it matters: Cherry oil was doing the heavy lifting for the non-sticky, skin-compatible finish that made the original a repurchase staple. Polyisobutene is fine — it's in half the market — but it's a lateral move at best and the $40 price didn't move laterally with it. The texture is noticeably tackier in the new formula.

The verdict: If you loved the original, this isn't it. The color range is genuinely good — Rosewood and Mahogany are beautiful sheers — but the base formula is now indistinguishable from options at half the price. Try the Rare Beauty Soft Pinch Tinted Lip Oil instead.

Check your current tube's batch code on checkcosmetic.net — if it was manufactured before March 2025, you have the old (better) formula. Stock up or move on.
