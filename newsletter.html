The Injection Report
An AI Can Now Spot Your Masseter Botox on a Routine Jaw Scan

What's going on: A preprint posted April 1 from a radiology team at Yonsei University describes a convolutional neural network trained on 4,200 panoramic dental radiographs that identifies masseter neuromodulator use with 91.3% accuracy. The model flags changes in muscle density and mandibular angle morphology that human radiologists miss consistently.

What it means: If you've been getting masseter Botox for TMJ or face slimming and checking "no" on the cosmetic procedure line at your dentist's office, that ambiguity is closing fast. Muscle atrophy from repeated neuromodulator use alters bone remodeling patterns, and AI is now better at reading those patterns than your oral surgeon.

What's next: Ask your injector how many units you're getting per side and start disclosing it on dental intake forms. Bone density shifts from chronic masseter treatment are clinically relevant for implant planning.

If you get masseter Botox more than twice a year, your dentist needs to know. Bone remodeling changes can affect implant success rates and bite alignment over time.

Put It In Your Mouth
Fermented Black Garlic Just Got Its First Serious Gut Trial

What's going on: A randomized controlled trial published March 31 in *Nutrients* out of Kyung Hee University gave 74 adults with mild functional dyspepsia 600mg of aged black garlic extract daily for 12 weeks. The fermented group showed a 38% reduction in bloating scores and significantly improved gut transit time versus placebo. The active compound, S-allyl-cysteine, appears to modulate gastric motility through serotonin receptor pathways in the gut lining.

What that means: This isn't raw garlic, which can torch a sensitive stomach. The fermentation process converts the harsh allicin into gentler sulfur compounds. If you deal with upper GI slowness, this is the first properly controlled evidence that aged black garlic does something beyond taste incredible on sourdough.

This Week's Cheat Meal

Look for "aged" or "fermented" black garlic extract standardized to S-allyl-cysteine. Raw garlic supplements are a completely different molecule with a completely different side effect profile.

Lip Lab
Glossier Just Quietly Swapped the Plasticizer in Lip Gloss

What's going on: Glossier updated its Lip Gloss formula this week with no announcement, swapping trimethylpentanyl succinate for a bio-derived polyglyceryl ester. Ingredient lists on Sephora updated April 2.

Why it matters: The old plasticizer gave that famously sticky, glass-finish pull. The new ester is what clean beauty formulators have been pushing for years, but it changes the texture. Less tack, more slip, slightly less shine. It's a regulatory hedge. The EU is tightening restrictions on certain succinate esters, and Glossier clearly wants one global formula.

The verdict: If you loved the original stick-to-everything texture, you'll notice. If you hated it, this is your re-entry point. The finish is more hydrating, less lacquered. Color payoff is identical.

Check the back of your tube. If polyglyceryl-2 isostearate appears in the first eight ingredients, you have the new formula.
