The Injection Report
Copper Peptides Are Gunning for Your Touch-Up Appointments

What's going on: A peer-reviewed study published this week in the *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* found that a 3% GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide) serum used twice daily for 12 weeks significantly improved fine lines in the perioral area — the exact zone where micro-dose Botox has dominated. The trial was small (68 participants) but double-blinded and placebo-controlled, which puts it ahead of most peptide claims.

What it means: GHK-Cu isn't new, but this is the first rigorous trial targeting lip-area lines specifically. The mechanism — stimulating collagen III and glycosaminoglycan synthesis — is complementary to neuromodulators, not competitive. Your injector isn't threatened; she's probably already stocking it for the between-appointment protocol.

What's next: Ask whether a copper peptide can slot into your maintenance routine between Botox cycles. If your injector hasn't read this study yet, send it to her — she'll respect you for it.

The study used 3% concentration applied to clean skin before any occlusive. Most OTC copper peptide serums are under 1%. Check the percentage before you assume yours qualifies.

Put It In Your Mouth
The FDA Wants Receipts From Your Collagen Powder

What's going on: The FDA published draft guidance this week proposing stricter substantiation requirements for protein-based dietary supplements — including collagen peptides. Brands would need to demonstrate bioavailability data, not just amino acid content, to make structure/function claims. Comment period opens July 21.

What that means: Most collagen powders currently market on total grams of protein, not on whether those peptides survive digestion intact enough to do anything. Brands with hydrolyzed marine or bovine collagen that have invested in pharmacokinetic studies will be fine. The $19 Amazon tub with "supports skin elasticity" on the label and no clinical data? That claim might disappear.

This week's cheat meal: The lobster roll at Luke's just came back in summer-weight form — chive butter, no mayo, on a split-top brioche. Collagen in its original packaging.

What to cut: If your collagen supplement doesn't list "hydrolyzed" or a specific peptide weight (under 5,000 daltons), it's probably not absorbing. This is a good week to audit.

This Week’s Cheat Meal
Look for supplements that list molecular weight of their peptides. Under 5,000 daltons is the threshold where absorption data actually exists. If the label doesn't mention it, the brand probably doesn't have the data.

Lip Lab
Merit Just Reformulated Its Lip Oil and It's a Whole New Product

What's going on: Merit quietly dropped a reformulated Shade Slick lip oil this week — new base, new finish, same packaging. The original launched in 2022 and was fine. This one is different.

Why it matters: They swapped the old coconut-derivative base for a meadowfoam-squalane blend, which means it actually absorbs into the lip tissue instead of sitting on top and migrating into your laugh lines by noon. The addition of bisabolol (chamomile-derived anti-inflammatory) is a nod to the lip-barrier conversation that's been building all year.

The verdict: Glossy without being goopy. Wears like a treatment, looks like a moment. The shade "Baby" is the one — sheer warm pink that makes you look like you just ate something expensive.

Meadowfoam seed oil has one of the highest oxidative stability ratings of any plant oil, meaning this formula won't go rancid in your hot car the way coconut-based lip oils do. Summer bag-proof.
