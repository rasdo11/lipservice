The Injection Report
Volux's Five-Year Data Is Here, and Duration Is the Story

What's going on: Allergan published five-year post-market surveillance data on Juvéderm Volux (HA filler for jawline and chin) this week in *Dermatologic Surgery*. The headline: 83% of patients retained clinically meaningful improvement at 24 months — but by year five, that number dropped to 41%, significantly lower than the brand's initial "up to 24 months" marketing implied.

What it means: Volux is the stiffest filler in the Juvéderm line (highest G prime), designed to hold structure along the jaw. The data confirms it works — but it also confirms what good injectors already suspected: mandibular anatomy metabolizes HA faster than, say, the cheek. Movement, mastication, and vascular density all accelerate breakdown. This isn't a failure; it's physics.

What's next: If you're due for a jawline touch-up, ask your injector whether they've adjusted their volume or layering strategy based on the new data. Some are already switching to a Volux-Voluma sandwich technique for longer hold.

Ask your injector: "Are you adjusting your jawline protocol based on the new Volux five-year data?" If they haven't read it, that's information too.

Put It In Your Mouth
The Watermelon-Citrulline Study That's Making Rounds Is Actually Legit

What's going on: A randomized controlled trial out of the University of Valencia, published this week in *Nutrients*, found that 6 weeks of daily watermelon juice (containing ~1.5g L-citrulline) significantly improved skin elasticity and hydration in women aged 30–50 versus placebo. The mechanism: citrulline converts to arginine, which boosts nitric oxide, which improves microcirculation to the skin.

What that means: This isn't the first citrulline-skin study, but it's the first with a whole-food delivery system and decent sample size (n=78). Your supplement stack just got competition from a fruit.

This week's cheat meal: A thick slice of high-fat burrata over grilled watermelon with flaky salt and chile crisp. Summer in one plate. Zero apologies.

Lip Lab
Dior Lip Oil Reformulated — and They Didn't Tell You

What's going on: Eagle-eyed beauty obsessives on r/MakeupAddiction flagged this week that Dior Addict Lip Glow Oil has a new INCI list. Cherry seed oil is out; meadowfoam seed oil is in. The texture is thinner. The scent is different.

Why it matters: Cherry seed oil was the hero emollient — high in oleic acid, great occlusivity. Meadowfoam is lighter and less expensive. The switch tracks with industry-wide reformulations driven by cherry kernel supply chain issues post-2024 drought seasons.

The verdict: Still a good lip oil. But if your holy grail suddenly feels different, you're not imagining it. The original formula was richer. This one layers better under lipstick but doesn't stand alone the same way.

If you stockpiled the old formula: it's not expired, it's vintage. If you're shopping fresh: test in store before repurchasing blind.
