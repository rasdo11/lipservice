The Injection Report
Allergan Reshuffles Allē and Your Botox Math Just Changed

What's going on: Allergan Aesthetics rolled out a restructured Allē loyalty program this week, adjusting point-to-dollar conversion rates and adding new tier thresholds that effectively raise the spend required to hit top rewards. Members who banked points under the old system are reporting lower redemption values on Botox and Juvéderm treatments starting immediately.

What it means: If you've been timing your filler appointments around point drops, recalculate. The new structure rewards frequency over bulk — patients who visit quarterly maintain status, but the twice-a-year crowd loses leverage. Your injector likely hasn't updated their front-desk script yet, so the information gap is real right now.

What's next: Ask your provider if they're an Allē Diamond practice — those offices get allocation perks that can offset your point erosion. And if you were sitting on points, redeem before the next conversion window closes July 31.

Ask your injector this week: "Are you Allē Diamond, and does that change what I can redeem?" The answer determines whether you book now or wait.

Put It In Your Mouth
High-Dose Omega-3s and Your Lip Barrier: The Study We've Been Waiting For

What's going on: A small but well-designed trial published this week in the *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* found that 2g/day of EPA-dominant omega-3 supplementation for 12 weeks significantly improved transepidermal water loss at the vermillion border — the technical name for where your lip meets your face. The control group using a standard lip balm alone showed no measurable barrier improvement.

What that means: Your lips don't have oil glands, which is why they're the first thing to crack. Systemic fatty acid supplementation does what topical occlusion can't — it changes the composition of the lipid matrix from the inside. This isn't about salmon twice a week; the therapeutic dose in the study was specific and high.

This week's cheat meal: A proper lobster roll from a proper shack. Warm butter, cold mayo, whichever denomination you follow — just make sure the ratio is obscene.

This Week’s Cheat Meal

Look for EPA-dominant omega-3s (not DHA-dominant). The study used a 3:1 EPA-to-DHA ratio at 2g total. Most gummies won't get you there — you need capsules or liquid.

Lip Lab
Merit's New Shade Range Is Gorgeous. The Ingredient List Has Questions.

What's going on: Merit dropped six new shades of its Signature Lip this week, expanding into deeper and cooler tones that the internet has been requesting since launch. Sold out twice on day one.

Why it matters: The formula includes polybutene high up on the INCI list — a synthetic hydrocarbon that gives that expensive-looking gloss but has been flagged by the EU's SCCS for purity concerns in cosmetic-grade sourcing. It's not banned, but it's worth knowing what's giving you that slip.

The verdict: The shade 'Marrakech' is genuinely stunning on medium-deep skin, the wear time is 3 hours realistic, and the finish is wet without being sticky. If polybutene doesn't bother you, this is the summer lip.

Polybutene gives slip and shine but quality varies wildly by supplier. If you're ingredient-cautious, ask Merit about their sourcing grade — or opt for a castor oil-based gloss instead.
