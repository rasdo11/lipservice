The Injection Report
Fat Is the New Filler for the Post-Ozempic Face

What's going on: A new retrospective published this week in *Aesthetic Surgery Journal* tracked 86 patients who underwent autologous fat grafting to address midface volume loss after significant GLP-1-mediated weight loss. Average fat survival at 12 months was 62% — meaningfully higher than the 40-50% historically cited, likely because these patients' weight had already stabilized. Several major practices, including Lara Devgan's NYC office, have begun marketing fat transfer specifically to this cohort.

What it means: Hyaluronic acid filler in large volumes carries migration and vascular risks that make surgeons nervous past about 4 syringes. Fat transfer sidesteps that entirely — it's your own tissue, it behaves like native fat, and it ages with you instead of metabolizing on its own timeline. The catch: it's a surgical procedure with real downtime (think 10-14 days of swelling), and results are operator-dependent in a way that filler isn't.

What's next: If you're on a GLP-1 and noticing hollowing, ask your provider whether you're a fat-transfer candidate *before* stacking filler. The consultation is the move right now, even if you're not ready to book.

The question to ask: "Am I better served by structural fat grafting than by adding more HA filler?" If your provider can't answer that, they shouldn't be treating volume loss at this scale.

Put It In Your Mouth
The Protein Ice Cream Wars Just Got Real Data

What's going on: A controlled feeding study out of the University of Toronto, published Monday in *The Journal of Nutrition*, compared satiety and muscle protein synthesis markers after isocaloric servings of three commercial high-protein ice creams (Halo Top, Nick's, and a new high-casein entry from Brave Robot) versus standard Häagen-Dazs. The high-casein formula outperformed on leucine bioavailability by 28% and kept participants fuller for 90 minutes longer than the whey-based options.

What that means: Not all protein ice cream is doing what you think it's doing. Whey spikes fast and drops; casein sustains. If you're eating it as a late-night protein hit — which, let's be honest, is the whole point — casein-based is the one that actually functions as advertised.

This week's cheat meal: The mortadella pizza at Don Angie in NYC — pistachio pesto, burrata, hot honey. An unserious amount of saturated fat served on a plate that makes you feel like a character in a novel.

Lip Lab
Glossier Quietly Reformulated Balm Dotcom and Nobody's Talking About It

What's going on: Eagle-eyed r/glossier users noticed this week that the INCI list on Balm Dotcom has changed — lanolin has been moved down the ingredient order, and a ceramide complex (ceramide NP + cholesterol + phytosphingosine) has been added. Glossier hasn't announced the reformulation.

Why it matters: The original formula was essentially fancy lanolin in a tube. The ceramide addition signals a shift toward barrier-repair positioning, which tracks with the brand's quiet pivot to skincare credibility post-2024 rebrand. Ceramides in a lip balm aren't gimmicky — compromised lip barrier is real, especially on tretinoin.

The verdict: If you bounced off old Balm Dotcom for being too waxy and not moisturizing enough, this is worth a retry. The texture is noticeably less occlusive. Berry still smells like a middle school sleepover and I mean that as a compliment.

If you're on tret or isotretinoin and your lips are wrecked, a ceramide lip balm is doing actual repair work — not just sealing in whatever moisture is left.
