Opening Statement
I threw out my entire skincare shelf.
It took eleven minutes and cost me $740.

Last Tuesday night, I stood in my bathroom with a trash bag and a flashlight on my phone, checking expiration dates on things I'd been putting on my face for longer than I'd like to admit. A vitamin C serum from 2023 that had turned the color of iced tea. A retinol I'd stored in direct sunlight like someone who has never once read a label. A jar of something French with no remaining legible text.

I told myself I was doing a "reset." In reality, I was doing the thing we all do when something in life feels out of control: reorganizing the smallest, most tactile corner of it. My face. My products. The ten inches of counter space I actually own.

There's a strange comfort in skincare purging. It lets you feel both virtuous and reckless. You are the scientist and the subject. You get to say "I'm starting fresh" without actually changing anything about your life except which acid you apply at 10 p.m. on a Wednesday.

Anyway. Welcome to today's Lip Service. We have brand drama, an underrated humectant, a runway moment worth stealing, and a charity doing work that actually matters. Grab your coffee. Let's go.

Brand Drama
Glossier quietly discontinues Skywash and Lidstar, and the subreddit is in mourning
No announcement, no farewell post, just a 404 page where Skywash used to live. Glossier has been trimming its lineup for months, but killing both liquid eye products at once feels like a statement: the brand is done pretending it wants to compete in color cosmetics. Insiders say a reformulated eye range is slated for fall, but "slated" is doing a lot of heavy lifting there.
Industry Moves
L'Oréal poaches Drunk Elephant's VP of Product Development
Mariana Chen, who helped architect Drunk Elephant's Protini reformulation and oversaw the brand's entry into body care, has joined L'Oréal's luxury division as SVP of Innovation. The hire signals L'Oréal's continued push to make its prestige tier feel more indie-credible. Shiseido, which owns Drunk Elephant, declined to comment.
Trend Alert
"Soap brows" are being replaced by something worse: "soap lashes"
TikTok's newest micro-trend involves brushing clear soap through your lashes before mascara for a fanned, clumpy, editorial look. Ophthalmologists are already begging people to stop. The tag has 14 million views, which in beauty math means it will be in a Sephora display by June.

Did You Know?
Polyglutamic acid is
quietly embarrassing hyaluronic acid.

You have probably seen polyglutamic acid lurking on ingredient lists for a year now, always in second or third position, never the headliner. That era is ending. PGA is a fermentation-derived humectant that holds four times more moisture than hyaluronic acid by weight, and the clinical data is finally catching up to the hype.

The Science, Briefly
Polyglutamic acid (γ-PGA) works by forming a moisture-retaining film on the skin's surface while simultaneously inhibiting the enzyme hyaluronidase, which breaks down your skin's natural hyaluronic acid. In short, it hydrates on contact and helps your body hold onto the hydration it already produces. One 2024 study in the Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology found a 20% improvement in transepidermal water loss after four weeks of twice-daily application.

The Ordinary launched a standalone PGA serum last fall that sold out in 72 hours. Laneige and Cosrx have been incorporating it into cushion compacts and essences for even longer, because K-beauty saw this coming in roughly 2019. If you have dry skin that drinks hyaluronic acid like it's nothing and still feels tight by noon, PGA is probably your answer. Here is the uncomfortable part: polyglutamic acid is not a miracle. It works best in humid environments and layered under an occlusive, which means if you live in Denver and refuse to use a moisturizer on top, this will not save you. Ingredients are tools, not religions. The best hydration strategy is still boring: multiple humectants, a solid occlusive, and drinking water like a person who respects their kidneys.

"PGA is not replacing hyaluronic acid. It is doing the job that HA was never designed to do alone, which is creating a sustained moisture reservoir on the skin surface." — Dr. Ranella Hirsch, Dermatologist, Boston

Did You See?
Loewe AW26 sent models out
in stained-glass lips
and nothing else on the face.
AW26 · Loewe's stained-glass lip in burgundy and amber

At Loewe's AW26 show in Paris last week, makeup artist Lucia Pica painted mouths in deep, translucent layers of burgundy, amber, and violet, building color that looked less like lipstick and more like light through cathedral windows. The rest of the face was bare, almost startlingly so. No concealer, no blush, visible texture everywhere. It was the kind of beauty look that makes you hold your breath because it trusts the audience to understand restraint. What Pica did was essentially the opposite of the "clean girl" face. Instead of distributing a little bit of perfection everywhere, she concentrated all the drama into one feature and let everything else be human. It is a philosophy Lip Service has been quietly championing: you do not need to finish the whole face. Sometimes one gorgeous, deliberate choice is the entire point.

Did You Help?
Beauty as an act
of dignity.

Beauty Bus Foundation was started in 2010 by Courtney Coates after she watched her father lose access to basic grooming during his battle with terminal illness. The Los Angeles-based nonprofit delivers free beauty and grooming services to chronically or terminally ill individuals and their caregivers, because feeling like yourself should not be a luxury that disappears when you get sick.

60,000+
free beauty services delivered to patients and caregivers since 2010

One of their volunteers described visiting a hospice patient who had not seen her own reflection in weeks and did not want to. After a haircut and a little color on her lips, she asked for a mirror. She said, "I look like me again." That sentence is the whole mission. Beauty Bus currently operates in Southern California and is actively fundraising to expand into three new metro areas this year. They need donations and licensed cosmetologists willing to volunteer.

Healthy Tears
Seventy-two thousand people
in a stadium.
One man with a broken piano
and twenty-one minutes.

By the summer of 1985, Freddie Mercury had spent fifteen years building the kind of career that made other rock stars feel like they were working in a smaller medium. Queen had already been written off at least twice by the British press. They were too theatrical, too campy, too much of everything. Mercury took every criticism as a stage direction.

Live Aid was not supposed to be Queen's moment. The concert at Wembley Stadium on July 13 was a global benefit for Ethiopian famine relief, featuring nearly every major act alive. Queen was given a slot, and they were told to keep it short. Bob Geldof's team had a schedule. There were satellites to coordinate, feeds to switch, a planet watching.

Mercury walked out in a white tank top and sat at a piano that was not fully in tune. What happened over the next twenty-one minutes is something music historians have spent four decades trying to explain and largely failing. He conducted 72,000 people like a choir. He improvised call-and-response sequences that the crowd followed without hesitation, as if they had rehearsed. He sprinted from one end of the stage to the other and never once looked like a man who had been told to cut things short. The performance was so commanding that other artists backstage reportedly stopped warming up and just watched the monitors.

He did not talk about what it meant afterward. He rarely explained anything. But there is a photograph taken from behind him on that stage, his fist raised, the crowd a blur of heat and light stretching to the horizon. Someone once asked Brian May what it felt like standing behind Freddie in that moment. May said simply, "It felt like he could have asked them to do anything, and they would have done it."

The performance every musician studies. You'll understand after 30 seconds.
